# Dimension 07: Education & Cultural Institution Data — Yala Province

## Research Date: June 2025
## Researcher: AI Deep-Dive Agent
## Scope: Comprehensive education data sources for Yala province with special attention to Islamic education (pondok schools) and education challenges

---

## Executive Summary

Yala province faces profound education challenges rooted in its unique sociopolitical context. The province ranks **25th nationally on the Human Achievement Index (HAI)** with a score of 0.7, but its **education dimension ranks 66th** — among the worst in Thailand. Only **44.2% of youth were enrolled in the formal education system in 2022** (vs. 79.3% nationally). The province has **500 educational institutions** comprising 334 schools, 147 local government education institutions, 9 district learning encouragement centres, 2 vocational institutions, 5 colleges, and 2 universities. Yala's **161 pondok institutions** (126 registered + 35 unregistered), **32 Islamic private schools**, and **19 Hafiz institutions** represent a significant parallel education system serving the Malay-Muslim majority population. The O-NET scores for Yala consistently fall **below national averages** (Yala average: 27.92 vs. national: 35.38 in 2025 for P.6).

## Critical Indicators for Dashboard Monitoring

### Priority 1: Urgent (Red Flag)
1. **O-NET scores** — consistently among lowest in Thailand
2. **Out-of-school children** — 12,802 in Yala alone (EEF)
3. **Upper secondary enrollment** — 69.2% vs. 79.3% national
4. **Youth NEET rate** — 30.8% (extremely high)
5. **Education HAI rank** — 66th nationally

### Priority 2: Important (Monitor)
6. **Dropout rate** — 560 in AY2018; family migration primary cause
7. **Teacher shortage** — especially in math, science, English
8. **Pondok registration** — 35 unregistered institutions
9. **Early childhood development** — stunting 23% vs. 13.3% national
10. **Alternative care quality** — no safeguarding policies in facilities
