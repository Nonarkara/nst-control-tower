# Facet: Southern Border Provinces Specific Data Sources

## Key Findings

- **Deep South Watch (DSW)** maintains four distinct databases: Conflict Incident Dataset (CID), Public Budget & Projects (PBP), Public Opinion & Participation (POP), and Socio-Economic/Education/Population (SEP), all with downloadable codebooks. The CID contains 15,000+ geocoded violent incidents dating from 2004 [^1^][^2^].
- **UNDP SDG Profiles** are available for Yala province (in Thai), developed collaboratively by UNDP, NIDA, TDRI, and Ministry of Interior. The profile covers UN SDG indicators aligned with Yala Provincial Development Plan (2022-2027) [^3^][^4^].
- **Yala City Municipality** operates a comprehensive public dashboard (yaladashboard.com) with real-time data on population (57,640 registered residents), budget (1,190.53 million THB for FY2025), service delivery, complaints, green space, carbon absorption, water supply, waste management, and PM2.5 levels [^5^][^6^].
- **IOM Thailand** conducted a scoping exercise across all 5 Southern Border Provinces (May-June 2024), collecting data on migrant communities, cross-border mobility, and access to services through 199 key informant interviews and visits to 23 points of entry [^7^].
- **Yala's GPP per capita** was 91,815 baht (2018), significantly below the national average of 236,815 baht, but higher than Narathiwat (62,066) and Pattani (75,997) [^8^].
- The **Southern Border Provinces Development Plan 2023-2027** aims to position the region as "A hub for agriculture, tourism, and border trade industries" with a combined gross product of approximately 139,173 million baht [^9^].
- **Betong district** in Yala has been designated as a **model sustainable development city**, with cross-border trade through the Betong-Pengkalan Hulu crossing reaching US$139 million in Thai exports (FY2013) [^10^][^11^].
- **Pondok educational institutions** in Yala: 126 registered, 35 unregistered (total 161), according to the Association of Pondok Educational Institutions. A 2024 survey identified 89 residential care facilities in Yala [^12^].
- **ACLED** (Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project) provides global conflict monitoring including southern Thailand, offering downloadable geocoded event data with weekly updates [^13^].

## Major Data Sources

### 1. Deep South Watch (DSW) / DSID (Deep South Incident Database)
- **URL**: https://deepsouthwatch.org/en (English), https://deepsouthwatch.org/th (Thai)
- **Codebook URL**: https://deepsouthwatch.org/en/node/12819
- **Data available**: Four primary datasets:
  - **DSW-CID**: Conflict Incident Dataset - 15,000+ geocoded violent incidents from January 2004 to present; includes incident type, date, location (with GIS coordinates), casualties by religion/occupation, perpetrators, targets
  - **DSW-PBP**: Public Budget & Projects Dataset - government budgets and projects related to southern border provinces
  - **DSW-POP**: Public Opinion & Participation Dataset - public attitudes and civic engagement
  - **DSW-SEP**: Socio-Economic, Education & Population Dataset - demographic and socioeconomic indicators
- **Geographic coverage**: Pattani, Yala, Narathiwat, Satun, and 4 districts of Songkhla (Chana, Na Thawi, Saba Yoi, Thepha)
- **Access method**: Monthly summary reports downloadable from website (PDF). Full database access requires direct contact with DSW/CSCD at Prince of Songkla University, Pattani Campus. Codebooks freely downloadable.
- **Update frequency**: Daily data entry, monthly editing/fact-checking, monthly reports published by 5th of following month
- **Data sources**: Emergency Call Center (Ministry of Interior, Yala-based), ISOC 4 daily reports, Southern Border Provinces Police Operation Center, regional media
- **Relevance to Yala**: Direct coverage of all incidents in Yala province; geocoded to village level; essential for municipal security planning and risk assessment

### 2. SBPAC (Southern Border Provinces Administrative Center) / สตช.
- **URL**: https://www.sbpac.go.th (primary); Office integrated with ISOC Region 4
- **Data available**: 
  - Development indicators for southern border provinces
  - KEADILAN (Justice) Center data - 326 administrative offices across 4 Deep South provinces + 4 Songkhla districts serving as government monitoring channels
  - Southern Border Provinces Development Plan 2023-2027 implementation data
  - Special Border Economic Corridor (SBEC) development data
  - Model city development tracking (Betong in Yala designated as model sustainable development city)
- **Geographic coverage**: Pattani, Yala, Narathiwat, Satun, and 4 districts of Songkhla
- **Access method**: Public reports through SBPAC website and government gazettes. Some data accessible through Information Disclosure Act requests. Direct coordination required for detailed datasets.
- **Update frequency**: Annual development reports, quarterly progress updates
- **Relevance to Yala**: Primary government body for regional development; manages Betong model city initiative; collects economic and social development data

### 6. ACLED (Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project)
- **URL**: https://acleddata.com/
- **Data available**: 
  - Individual event records: battles, explosions/remote violence, violence against civilians, protests, riots, strategic developments
  - Each record includes: date, location (with coordinates), actors involved, fatalities, event notes
  - Thailand coverage includes southern border provinces
  - From 2010-2015, ACLED recorded 1,296 incidents in the Patani region [^13^]
- **Geographic coverage**: Global, including Thailand's southern border provinces
- **Access method**: Free registration required at acleddata.com; bulk download in CSV/Excel; API available for programmatic access
- **Update frequency**: Weekly updates with events coded within approximately 1 week
- **Relevance to Yala**: Alternative/secondary source for violence monitoring; useful for cross-referencing with DSID data

### 7. Prince of Songkla University - Southern Border Provinces Research Database
- **URL**: https://soreda.oas.psu.ac.th/
- **Data available**: 
  - Research database covering Pattani, Yala, Narathiwat, and Satun
  - Topics: politics/governance, arts/culture, way of life/traditions, history, landscape/tourism, natural resources/environment, education, health, religion, law
  - Initially developed with National Research Council of Thailand funding
- **Geographic coverage**: Pattani, Yala, Narathiwat, Satun
- **Access method**: Web-based search portal (Thai language)
- **Update frequency**: Ongoing since 2007; expanded in 2011
- **Relevance to Yala**: Academic research syntheses on Yala-specific topics; useful for context and qualitative data

## Security & Incident Data

### Deep South Watch / DSID (Detailed)
- **Methodology**: Four-source data triangulation:
  1. Emergency Call Center (Ministry of Interior, Yala-based) - daily reports
  2. ISOC 4 (Internal Security Operations Command Region 4) - daily military reports
  3. Southern Border Provinces Police Operation Center - crime/incident data
  4. Regional media centers - news reports
- **Data processing**: Daily collection, triangulation across sources, coding/entry, monthly editing and fact-checking, GIS geocoding using ArcView with satellite imagery from GISTDA and Google Earth
- **Quality control**: Two-stage quality control implemented since DSID 2.0 (2014)
- **GIS capability**: 15,000+ incidents geocoded to village level using ISO-standard administrative codes
- **Access restrictions**: Full database requires academic/government partnership or direct negotiation with CSCD/DSW. Monthly summary reports publicly available.
- **Contact**: Center for Conflict Studies and Cultural Diversity (CSCD), Prince of Songkla University, Pattani Campus

### Southern Border Provinces Police Operation Center (ศชต.)
- **Data available**: Crime statistics, bomb incident data, car bomb statistics, violent incident summaries
- **Access method**: Data published indirectly through Isranews (https://www.isranews.org/south-news/) and Thai PBS; official requests through Royal Thai Police
- **Key statistics available**: Annual bomb counts, violent deaths/injuries, incident trends by type
- **Relevance**: Primary source for law enforcement data in the region; data feeds into DSW

### ISOC 4 (Internal Security Operations Command Region 4)
- **URL**: https://thainews.prd.go.th/ (indirect - through government news)
- **Data available**: Daily security reports on incidents across Pattani, Yala, Narathiwat; progress reports on investigations
- **Access method**: Classified operational data; limited public reporting through government channels and news releases
- **Geographic coverage**: ISOC Region 4 Forward Command headquartered in southern provinces (Pattani) [^14^]
- **Relevance**: Primary source for DSW data triangulation; direct access restricted

## Trends & Signals

- **Conflict trends**: Violence incidents show long-term decline from peak of 2,475 events (2007) to significantly lower levels by 2024, though episodic attacks continue [^16^]. Deep South Watch recorded 23,406 incidents and 22,508 casualties from 2004 to October 2025 [^14^].
- **Demographic trends**: Yala province population grew from 511,911 (2014) to 532,326 (2018), a growth rate of ~1% annually [^15^]. Yala City Municipality population has slightly declined from 61,218 (2018) to 57,640 (2025) per dashboard data.
- **Economic trends**: GPP per capita in Yala (91,815 THB, 2018) remains far below national average (236,815 THB), but above neighboring Narathiwat and Pattani [^8^]. Southern Border Provinces Development Plan 2023-2027 targets BCG economic model implementation.
- **Smart City development**: Yala City Municipality has adopted Smart City initiatives with digital dashboard, free WiFi (47 points), online complaint systems, and LINE OA citizen engagement [^5^].
- **Cross-border infrastructure**: Betong district designated as model sustainable development city with plans for airport, industrial estate, and upgraded customs facilities [^11^].
- **Migrant population**: IOM study found limited but significant non-Thai populations in Yala, primarily working in agriculture, fishing, and rubber wood industries [^7^].

## Controversies & Limitations

- **Data sensitivity**: Security incident data is politically sensitive. ISOC 4 and police data are classified; public access is limited to aggregated summaries. IOM reports are marked "FOR RESTRICTED DISTRIBUTION ONLY DUE TO SENSITIVE CONTENT" [^7^].
- **Under-reporting**: ACLED analysis confirms significant under-reporting of violence in global datasets compared to local sources like DSID. DSID captured 2.7 times as many deaths as UCDP-GED in 2014, attributed to DSID's access to local military/police reports and Thai-language media [^17^].
- **Access restrictions**: Full DSW/DSID database access requires institutional partnerships; individual researchers must negotiate access. SBPAC operational data is largely internal.
- **Data quality concerns**: Cause of incidents is increasingly recorded as "unclear/unidentifiable" - rising from 20% (2004-2019 average) to 44% in 2018, suggesting either genuine ambiguity or classification sensitivity [^18^].
- **Conflict vs. crime distinction**: Disentangling insurgency-related violence from ordinary crime and personal conflicts remains methodologically challenging [^1^].
- **Migrant data gaps**: Systematic data on migrant populations in Yala is extremely limited due to security constraints and the novelty of IOM engagement in the area [^7^].
- **Language barriers**: Many datasets and reports are available only in Thai, limiting international accessibility.

## Recommended Deep-Dive Areas

1. **DSW-CID Full Database Access**: Negotiate data-sharing agreement with CSCD/DSW for geocoded incident data at sub-district/tambon level for Yala City Municipality area. The codebook and methodology are well-documented; this would provide the most granular security data available. **Warranted because**: Essential for municipal risk mapping and safety planning.

2. **UNDP Yala SDG Profile Deep Analysis**: The full Thai-language SDG profile for Yala needs to be systematically extracted for all indicators relevant to municipal management (Goals 1, 3, 4, 6, 7, 8, 9, 11, 13, 16). **Warranted because**: Only comprehensive standardized development baseline specifically for Yala province.

3. **Yala City Municipality Dashboard API/Data Integration**: The municipal dashboard (yaladashboard.com) appears to have downloadable datasets for each indicator. These should be systematically downloaded and integrated. **Warranted because**: Only real-time municipal-level data source with transparency-grade public access.

4. **IOM Yala Scoping Report Detailed Extraction**: Despite restricted distribution, the Yala-specific report contains granular data on migrant communities, POE assessments, and service gaps that is unavailable elsewhere. **Warranted because**: First and only systematic migrant population data for Yala.

5. **Cross-Border Trade - Betong Real-Time Data**: The Department of Foreign Trade should have updated trade statistics through Betong crossing. ADB's SBEZ scoping study is from 2015; current trade volumes are needed. **Warranted because**: Betong is Yala's primary economic gateway and designated model city.

6. **Pondok Education System Data**: The 126+ registered pondok institutions in Yala represent a parallel education system serving a significant portion of the Muslim population. Enrollment data, curriculum, and geographic distribution would inform education planning. **Warranted because**: Cultural institutions are central to Yala's social fabric but poorly documented in official statistics.

7. **SBPAC Development Project Database**: SBPAC's KEADILAN centers and development projects generate implementation data that could be accessed through official information requests. **Warranted because**: Primary government development agency for the region.
