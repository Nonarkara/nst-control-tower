## Executive Summary

Yala City Municipality (เทศบาลนครยะลา) operates one of Thailand's most advanced local government digital infrastructures. Under Mayor Pongsak Yingchoncharoen's leadership since 2019, the municipality has built **YALA Resilience City** - a comprehensive smart city ecosystem anchored by dual dashboards (Mayor Dashboard + Citizen Dashboard at yaladashboard.com), 5 integrated Big Data systems developed in partnership with Bedrock Analytics, multi-channel citizen engagement (LINE OA @yalacity, Yala Mobile App, Facebook Messenger chatbot), and open data publishing on DEPA's citydata.in.th platform.

The municipality has won the **DGA Digital Local Government Award (DGTi) for 3 consecutive years** (2022, 2023, 2024) and achieved an ITAS transparency score of 94.21 (Grade A). However, significant data gaps remain in areas including real-time transportation, economic/trade data, building permit digitization completeness, water billing integration, and cross-municipal data coordination with Betong District.

### 10.3 Historical Notes

Yala city has been relocated 4 times historically. The current city was planned during the reign of Phra Rattakit Wijarn (Sawat Na Nakhon), the 10th governor of Yala Province (1913-1915), who established "kilometer zero" and laid out the city in concentric circles connected by spider-web roads - known as Thailand's most beautiful spider-web road pattern.

## 18. Summary of Key Findings

### Strengths
1. **Mature dashboard infrastructure** - 19+ data modules across 4 categories
2. **Award-winning digital governance** - 3 consecutive DGA awards
3. **Comprehensive data collection** - Drone, MMS, manual, and citizen-reported data
4. **Multi-channel citizen engagement** - Web, LINE OA, Mobile App, Facebook
5. **Open data commitment** - 15 datasets on DEPA platform
6. **Proven technology partner** - Bedrock Analytics partnership delivering results
7. **Strong executive leadership** - Mayor personally driving digital transformation

### Weaknesses
1. **IT staffing bottleneck** - All IT functions in one division unit
2. **No dedicated IT budget** - Embedded in general operations
3. **Transportation data absent** - Public transport module non-functional
4. **Economic data missing** - No business or trade statistics
5. **System silos persist** - Water billing on separate platform
6. **No predictive analytics** - Dashboard is descriptive only
7. **Limited cross-municipal coordination** - Betong, Yala PAO not integrated

### Opportunities
1. **Transportation IoT deployment** - Real-time public transport tracking
2. **Economic dashboard module** - Business registration and market data
3. **Water billing integration** - API connection to existing system
4. **Tourism data collection** - Visitor analytics for Yala city
5. **Regional data hub** - Expand YALA Resilience City to cover Betong
6. **Predictive analytics** - Budget forecasting, population projection
7. **NSO/DGA data integration** - Connect to national open data portals

### Threats
1. **Staff turnover** - Loss of key IT personnel would cripple systems
2. **External dependency** - Heavy reliance on Bedrock Analytics for data/AI
3. **Sustainability** - Award momentum may not translate to continued investment
4. **Security** - Multiple touchpoints (LINE, App, Web) increase attack surface
5. **Data quality** - Citizen-reported data (complaints) may have bias/inaccuracy
