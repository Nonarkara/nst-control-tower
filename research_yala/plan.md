# Plan: Yala Municipality Data Source Bible

## Objective
Create a comprehensive "bible of sources" — APIs, open datasets, government portals, geographic data, and information resources — that the City Municipality of Yala (Southern Thailand) should integrate into their municipal database and geographic dashboard for data-driven decision making.

## Stage 1 — Deep Research (Parallel Swarm)
Load `deep-research-swarm` skill. Deploy multiple parallel research agents covering:

1. **Thailand National Open Data & Government APIs** — data.go.th, government open data portals, Bangkok-centric municipal data systems that Yala can mirror
2. **Yala Provincial & Local Government Data Sources** — Provincial Administrative Organization, Yala City Municipality own data, local statistics offices
3. **Geographic & GIS Data Sources** — Royal Thai Survey Department, GISTDA, OpenStreetMap, satellite imagery, land use, transportation networks
4. **Demographic, Economic & Social Data** — NSO census data, household surveys, poverty data, education, health, crime, religious/cultural demographics specific to Southern Border Provinces
5. **Infrastructure & Utilities Data** — water, electricity, waste management, transportation, telecommunications
6. **International & Regional Open Data Sources** — World Bank, UN ESCAP, ASEAN, climate data, disaster management
7. **Existing Dashboard Platforms & Reference Architectures** — Thai smart city initiatives, municipal GIS dashboards in Thailand, technical frameworks

## Stage 2 — Report Writing
Load `report-writing` skill. Synthesize all research into a structured, actionable source bible organized by:
- Data category / domain
- Source name & URL
- Access method (API, download, manual request)
- Data available & update frequency
- Relevance to Yala Municipality
- Integration priority (must-have / should-have / nice-to-have)

## Stage 3 — Formatting
Load `docx` skill. Convert final markdown to professionally formatted Word document.
